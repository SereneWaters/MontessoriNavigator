Pink Tower

Material: A set of pink cubes in different sizes.

Prerequisites: The child should have experience with block stacking.

Literal Goal: Stack the cubes from largest to smallest.

Developmental Goal: Develop visual discrimination of size and coordination.

Point of Attention: The child's focus should be on placing each block carefully.

Steps:

  1. Invite the child and introduce the material.
  2. Demonstrate stacking the cubes one by one.
  3. Allow the child to try independently.

Age: 2.5 - 3 years

Vocabulary: Large, small, bigger, smaller, cube.

Common Errors: Misplacing blocks, skipping sizes.

Error Correction: Encourage the child to compare cube sizes.

Alternative: Let the child arrange cubes in a line instead of stacking.
